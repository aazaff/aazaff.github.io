Andrew Zaffos

My personal research follows two basic thrusts. First, I am interested in the extinction and diversification of marine organisms, and how patterns of stratigraphic turnover have controlled diversity throughout the Phanerozoic by restructuring habitats and/or creating artefactual gaps in the fossil record. Second, I am interested in changes in the geographic and environmental distribution of organisms, either regionally along onshore-offshore transects of the continental shelf or globally along continental shorelines. Particularly when such changes are driven or concomitant with changes in diversity or stratigraphic turnover. I am also a member of the University of Wisconsin's Macrostratigraphy Lab under Shanan E. Peters. In particular, I work on group projects utilizing the Macrostrat Database, the Paleobiology Database, and the GeoDeepDive initiative.
